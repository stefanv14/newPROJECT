GREAT PRODUCTS

Smoothies are a great way to pack fiber- and vitamin-rich fruits and vegetables, protein, and good fats into a drinkable meal or snack. They're really easy, fast, and cheap to make at home, and you can create lots of flavor combinations using what you have. Store-bought smoothies can contain more added sugar than you may want, so making your smoothies at home lets you control how sweet they are too. With a the simple smoothie formula and tips below, you can make a healthy smoothie at home no matter which combination of ingredients you use.

Peanut Butter Banana Smoothie

It is so refreshing and it's sweet and tasty

"2" bananas, broken into chunks "2" cups milk "1/2" cup peanut butter "2" tablespoons honey, or to taste "2" cups ice cubes

Healthy Breakfast Smoothie

"1/2" cup spinach, or more to taste1 frozen banana, cut into chunks "1/2" small avocado, peeled and pitted "1/4" cup almond milk "1/2" teaspoon vanilla extract "1/4" teaspoon ground cinnamon

Carrot Cake Smoothie

"1" large carrot, peeled and diced "1/4" cup frozen mango chunks "1" large fresh peach, chopped "1/4" cup soy milk "1" tablespoon ground cinnamon "1" teaspoon ground allspice "1" teaspoon ground ginger
